Signs It's Time to Replace Your Furnace

Outdated Tech

Heating technology improves fast. A modern, efficient furnace with smart controls heats better and costs less to run than an older unit.

High Repair Costs

When repairs on an old furnace approach the cost of a new one — scarce parts, heavy wear — replacement is the better value.

Aging Unit

Furnaces need more repairs as they age. Most should be replaced every 15 to 20 years; a newer model cuts bills and upkeep.

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I should replace instead of repair?

If your furnace is 15+ years old, needs frequent or costly repairs, or cannot hold temperature, replacement usually saves money over time. We will give you an honest recommendation.

Will a new furnace lower my energy bills?

Yes — modern high-efficiency furnaces convert far more fuel into heat, which typically means noticeably lower winter bills.

Are rebates available on a new furnace?

Often, yes. Manufacturers and utilities regularly offer rebates on high-efficiency models — we will help you find what applies.
